Tips for Navigating Your Journey

How to Deal with an Overwhelming Information Load

  • Consider bringing a friend or loved one: Having someone with you can help you remember all the important information shared during your appointment.
  • Prepare in advance: Learning about your specific condition from trusted sources beforehand can help you make the most of your appointment time.
  • We welcome your questions: It's common to find conflicting information. We are here to help you sort through it and understand what applies to you. Don't hesitate to ask to clarify anything you're unsure about.

How to Get Expert Opinions from Your Healthcare Team

  • A team of experts is available to you: Different members of your healthcare team have different areas of expertise. Don't hesitate to ask doctors, nurses, social workers, patient advocates, integrative care specialists and others, learn more here.
  • Your questions are important: Write down your questions for your nurse and doctor. Sharing them at the beginning of the appointment will help ensure they're all addressed.
  • Stay connected between visits: Remember, you can reach your healthcare team between appointments through MyChart, by phone, or sometimes even by email. Don't hesitate to reach out when questions arise.

As You Gather Information, Consider:

As you research therapies, medicines, supplements, or procedures, it's important to evaluate their potential benefits and risks. Here's what to consider:

  • Relevance: Does this information apply to my specific situation and symptoms?
  • Proven Benefits: How many people have actually benefited, and is this information from a reliable source (not just testimonials)? Look for numbers and scientific studies.
  • Scientific Backing: Is this treatment supported by research in reputable medical journals like those found on PubMed? Ask a librarian for help if needed.
  • Credible Source: Is the author a qualified expert with verifiable credentials?
  • Realistic Claims: Be wary of promises of quick, easy miracles. Effective treatments rarely sound too good to be true.

Emotional Support is a Critical Part of Your Care

  • Connect with others: Joining a support group, especially one affiliated with a healthcare center, can be incredibly helpful. Sharing experiences with others facing similar challenges can provide valuable support and insights. Consider joining one early in your journey.
  • Seek emotional support: Don't hesitate to reach out to social workers or patient advocates. They can connect you with resources that offer emotional support and guidance during this time. They're there to help.
